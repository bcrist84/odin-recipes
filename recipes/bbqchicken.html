Delicious BBQ Chicken

Growing up I loved eating bbq chicken. It was something of a holiday tradition. I would go from Memorial day to 4th of July waiting for some bbq chicken. Now that I'm all grown I no longer have to wait for a holiday and cook it quite often.

This is a very simple recipe using chicken thighs and bbq sauce. Cooking low and slow on a grill. I use a pellet grill. I love it for it's ease of temperature set and smokey flavor it adds.

I buy the packs of 10 boneless chicken thighs. If you have a big bowl throw them in and take a half cup of your favorite bbq sauce, throw that in. Toss the chicken til they are all coated and let sit. Fire up the grill to 225 degrees. If you are using smoke to add more flavor, this is a good time to get that going too. Now that the grill is all ready. Grab the chicken and lay it out on the grill. I put bbq sauce in a bowl with a bbq brush. Every 20 mins I apply bbq sauce then flip the chicken. Repeat, til meat thermometer reads 165 degrees then pull chicken from grill and let rest for 5 to 10 mins.

Ingredients

  • Package of Chicken Thighs
  • Your Favorite BBQ Sauce

Steps

  1. Pull chicken out of package
  2. Rinse chicken and pat dry
  3. Place chicken and puor 1/2 cup bbq sauce into bowl/container
  4. Toss chicken til fully coated with bbq sauce
  5. Place chicken to the side
  6. (optional) Marinate chicken in bbq sauce
  7. Prepare the grill to 225 degrees
  8. (optional) Prepare smoking method
  9. Place chicken on grill apply bbq sauce to tops
  10. Cook for 20 mins then flip and apply bbq sauce to tops
  11. Repeat until internal temp is 165 degrees
  12. Pull chicken from grill loosly cover with tin foil
  13. Allow chicken to rest for 5 to 10 mins
  14. Time to eat!
