Juicy Half Pound Hamburgers

This is a juicy one. I love hamburgers, big thick burgers. I make nothing less than a half pound burger. It cooks to the perfect doneness for me and remains rediculously juicy. They also reheat well keeping juicy.

For this recipe. I don't measure anything, its all by eyeballing it. Because of this the flavors for the burgers do vary from make to make. I might eventually measure out ingredients. So the flavor is more consistent. When I do the ingredients list will be updated with amounts.

Lets get started. I start out with 2lbs of burger. It's your choice of cut of meat and fat percentage. I go with either 80-20 ground beef. Or a prime cut of meat ground into burger. Place the burger into a big bowl to mix. The seasonings that I mix into the burger are cayenne pepper becuase I like a little kick of spice. Paprika, chile powder, garlic (minced, granules, or powder), ground pepper, salt, and worcestershire sauce. If I'm not topping the burger with onions. I will add in onion (granules, or powder). Mix everything really well and form into half pound patties. I use a food scale for accurate weight. Now lets get the grill ready. We will be grilling these at a low temp of 225 degrees. If you want even more flavor we can add in a source of smoke since it is a slower cook. When the grill is heated up, throw the burgers on. We wont flip the burgers any during the cook only at the sear. It's really handy to have a meat thermometer. I use a Thermapen and really like it. Once your burgers are close to the disired doneness. It is time to finish with a sear. Crank the heat up and sear one side thnn the other, putting cheese on as soon as you start searing the final side. Remove from grill and let rest for a few minutes before digging into these flavorful hamburgers.

Ingredients

  • 2lbs ground beef (higher fat better)
  • Worcestershire sauce
  • Cayenne pepper
  • Chile powder
  • Paprika
  • Garlic (minced,granules, or powder)
  • Ground Pepper
  • Salt
  • (optional) Onion (powder, granules)
  • Sliced cheese of choice
  • Buns of choice
  • Condiments and toppings of choice

Steps

  1. Prepare grill to 225 degrees
  2. (optional) Prepare smoke source
  3. Mix ground beef, worcestershire sauce, and spices in large bowl
  4. Form into 1/2 pound patties
  5. Place patties on grill
  6. Cook til internal temp is close to desired doneness
  7. Sear both sides of hamburger at a high temp add cheese to burger as final side is being seared
  8. Pull from grill and rest under tinfoil tent for 5 minutes
  9. Place on bun and dress to your liking
  10. Time to eat!
